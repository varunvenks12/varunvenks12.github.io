Kumaraparvatha is the fourth highest peak in Karnataka (5,617 ft/ 1712 m), after Tadiandamol Peak (5735 ft / 1748 m). However, this is named first and highest in terms of trek.

History of Kumaraparvatha: The divine serpent Vasuki whom Lord Shiva wore as an ornament around his neck, was threatened by Garuda ( mount/Vahana of Lord Vishnu). Vasuki performed tapas (worship) for numerous years in the caves of Kukke Subhramaniya temple trying to avoid attacks from Garuda. Lord Shiva decided to send his son Kartikeya to protect Vasuki from Garuda. Apparently Kartikeya visited Kumara Parvatha and blessed Vasuki that he shall stay with him forever. From then onwards the pooja offered to serpent shall be considered as the pooja offered to Kartikeya himself.

PLANNING

The plan was initiated during the last week of November and were seven of usâ€”four who had previously trekked KP, and three who were new to the experience. Considering year end leaves, we planned to reserve transportation well in advance. We preferred the bus over the train because the bus reached the destination earlier than the train. Reserved onward and return bus tickets. Carried breakfast and lunch for a day. Shopped tablets, snacks, energy drinks and bites. Booked camping tents in advanced and carried from Bangalore.

EXECUTION

Started our journey towards Kukke Subramanya from Bangalore KSRTC bus stand at 10:15 PM.

Day 1, 31 Dec:- Reach Bhatrumane (Girigadde) and halt (5.5 KMs):
  • Reached Kukke Subramanya at 5:15 AM. We finished freshening up and breakfast at 7:40 AM.
  • To reach the trek entry point its a 1 KM walk from the temple. At the forest office, all our bag packs, plastics are checked.
  • Note: In this recent development, there is a requirement to account for all the single-use plastics covers, bags, socks, medicine sachets with a mandatory deposit for each item. Under each individual's name, all the above mentioned things are documented on the receipt they provide. Ensure to hand over the receipt when returning. Additionally, the use of flags, knives, matchboxes, speakers, drones is strictly prohibited.
  • When we enquired the officers about the approximate count of trekkers as on 30th, they reported around 950 people. And as on 31st, around 150-200.
  • By 8:40 AM all our bags were checked and we started ascending. Took some pictures, videos en-route.
  • At 10:40 AM, we reached a mid-point where there's a waterfall which is 100m diversion from the actual route. We filled our water bottles, took some pictures and continued towards Girigadde.
  • At 1:00 PM, we reached Bhatrumane, At first sight, we saw a crowd with plates waiting for lunch, Many of those who made it to peak the same day and to descend back to Kukke Subramanya.
  • Although we were concerned about finding a camping site, we had to fill our stomachs first, therefore we waited until the first crowd to finish lunch. By 2:15 PM we completed our meal and started towards forest office at Girigadde.
  • We reported to the forest officer with head count and with the tents that we carried. They suggested a place to pitch our tents.
    Note: When reporting to forest officer, you are suppose to submit a valid ID proof number, address and signature on the form they provide.
  • By 3:15PM, we had both the tents set up. That was all for the day. We had enough time to rest until 5:30PM.
  • At 5:30 PM, we all headed to Kumaraparvatha First View Point to witness the sunset, took some pictures, made calls to family members. Upon reaching the camp, had snacks and engaged in chit-chat. We were lazy to walk to Bhatrumane for dinner and a very few went to have dinner.
  • The day winded up early at 9:00 PM. We were already listening the noise of new year celebration in other camps. We were all set to wake up at 4:00 AM and the permissible trek start time is 5:00 AM.
